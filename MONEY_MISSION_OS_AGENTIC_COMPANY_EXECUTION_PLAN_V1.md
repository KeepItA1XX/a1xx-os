# Money Mission OS — Agentic Company Execution Plan v1

**Status:** Agreed planning baseline
**Owner:** A1XX / Executive
**Operating principle:** one bounded proof at a time; no autonomy before truth, ownership, permissions, and verification.

## Outcome

Build a daily-use, human-directed, agent-operated company system in which A1XX receives a truthful brief, focuses on founder-only decisions, and trusts governed agents to prepare, route, execute, and report approved work.

## Phase 1 — Lock Strategic Target v2 and decision rights

**Goal:** convert research into the official operating model.

**Outputs:** Strategic Target v2; decision-rights matrix; approval matrix; company operating principles; first three workflow choices.

**Decisions required from A1XX:**

- first revenue workflow;
- first fulfillment workflow;
- first content workflow;
- authoritative system for each core record;
- actions allowed without approval;
- actions that always require approval;
- proactive notification thresholds;
- treatment of personal versus business context.

**Gate:** no profile implementation beyond draft documentation until these decisions are recorded.

## Phase 2 — Build the primary leadership profiles

### Chief of Staff

Owns company rhythm, cross-functional routing, risk detection, decision preparation, operating dashboards, and closure of handoffs.

### Executive Assistant

Owns founder attention, calendar and commitment integrity, preparation, reminders, information triage, and breadcrumbs.

**Outputs for each profile:**

- mission and non-mission;
- thinking model;
- inputs and authoritative sources;
- tools and API permissions;
- staff and worker map;
- outputs and destinations;
- approval rules;
- escalation behavior;
- daily/weekly cadence;
- quality scorecard;
- evaluation scenarios;
- version and rollback record.

**Gate:** both agents can explain what they own, what they do not own, when they ask, and where every output goes.

## Phase 3 — Define the shared operating contracts

**Goal:** prevent isolated agent offices from becoming competing companies.

Create the contracts for:

- canonical records;
- domain workspace overlays;
- governed memory and freshness;
- agent identity and role mailboxes;
- tool/API health;
- permission tiers;
- handoffs;
- approval requests;
- receipts and audit trails;
- evaluation and promotion.

Every workspace must show source, owner, status, freshness, confidence, next action, destination, approval state, and receipt ID.

**Gate:** no new agent receives production write access until its contract passes review.

## Phase 4 — Prove three business workflows

Run exactly one workflow in each lane:

1. Revenue — lead/outreach/offer/follow-up.
2. Fulfillment — project/member/client delivery.
3. Content — idea/script/asset/publishing preparation.

Each proof must demonstrate:

- live source read;
- clear owner;
- bounded agent job;
- correct tool/API access;
- approval pause;
- correct export destination;
- verified completion;
- duplicate prevention;
- failure and recovery behavior;
- durable receipt;
- founder-visible next action.

**Gate:** Daily Use Readiness v1 requires all three proofs to pass in repeated runs, not just one successful demo.

## Phase 5 — Activate proactive Mission Command carefully

Enable in this order:

1. read-only change alerts;
2. draft recommendations;
3. approval requests;
4. approved task creation;
5. approved routing;
6. approved execution;
7. verified completion reporting.

Mission Command must proactively surface changes, risks, stalled work, external waiting, missing approvals, and next-best actions. It must not create noise or make silent commitments.

**Gate:** every proactive alert must have a reason, source, urgency, owner, suggested action, and suppression/deduplication rule.

## Phase 6 — Release and expand

Release only after:

- live reads and unavailable states pass;
- approval and destination routing pass;
- agent simulations pass;
- evaluation/regression suite passes;
- browser and responsive QA pass;
- proactive alerts are useful and non-duplicative;
- receipts and rollback are verified;
- A1XX can operate the day without reconstructing state manually.

Then expand one domain captain and its staff at a time, based on the largest measured bottleneck.

## Standing weekly rhythm

**Monday:** Chief of Staff prepares priorities, risks, and capacity view.

**Daily morning:** Executive Assistant prepares A1XX brief; Chief of Staff reconciles company state.

**Daily operating window:** agents execute only within approved contracts; exceptions escalate.

**Daily closeout:** reconcile completed, waiting, blocked, approved, and stale work.

**Weekly review:** evaluate outcomes, corrections, cost, latency, duplicates, escalation quality, and founder time saved.

## Success measures

- founder time spent reconstructing status;
- percentage of work with clear owner and destination;
- first-pass acceptance rate;
- correction and duplicate rate;
- unsupported-claim rate;
- approval turnaround time;
- verified completion rate;
- proactive alert precision;
- external waiting time;
- revenue and fulfillment throughput;
- agent cost and latency;
- number of unresolved ownership conflicts.

## Stop conditions

Pause expansion when:

- source truth is stale or conflicting;
- an agent acts outside permission;
- an output has no verified destination;
- duplicate or missing work appears;
- approval becomes rubber-stamp behavior;
- agent count grows faster than measurable business capacity;
- a role begins making strategic decisions reserved for A1XX.

## Immediate next action

Prepare Strategic Target v2 and the decision-rights matrix, then draft the Chief of Staff and Executive Assistant profiles against those locked decisions. No production implementation is implied by this planning document.
